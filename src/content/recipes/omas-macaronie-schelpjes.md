## Ingredients

### For the sauce
- 30 grams butter
- 30 grams flour
- 200 ml milk (at room temperature)
- Pepper to taste
- 1 teaspoon Maggi seasoning (or soy sauce)

### For the filling
- 150 grams macaroni (or leftover pasta)
- 100 grams diced ham
- 75 grams grated cheese (e.g. Gouda)

### For the topping
- 2 tablespoons breadcrumbs
- 25 grams grated cheese
- 1 knob of butter (per shell)

## Equipment

- Saucepan for the sauce
- Pot for cooking macaroni
- Whisk or wooden spoon
- 4 oven-safe shell dishes or small baking dishes
- Baking tray or oven dish

## Instructions

1. **Preheat the oven** to 175Â°C (350Â°F).

2. **Cook the macaroni** (if not using leftovers): Cook the macaroni according to package instructions in plenty of salted water until al dente. Drain and set aside.

3. **Make the roux**: Melt the butter in a saucepan over low heat. Add the flour and stir with a whisk until smooth. Let it cook gently for 1-2 minutes (don't let it brown).

4. **Make the sauce**: Gradually add the milk while stirring continuously. Keep stirring until you have a smooth, thick sauce. This takes about 5 minutes.

5. **Season the sauce**: Add the pepper and Maggi seasoning. Taste and adjust the seasoning if needed.

6. **Add the filling**: Stir the diced ham and 75 grams of grated cheese into the sauce until the cheese has melted.

7. **Combine with macaroni**: Fold the cooked macaroni into the sauce. Gently stir until everything is well combined.

8. **Fill the shells**: First sprinkle a layer of breadcrumbs on the bottom of the shells. Then divide the macaroni mixture on top.

9. **Add the topping**: Sprinkle each shell with grated cheese and place a knob of butter on top.

10. **Bake in the oven**: Place the shells on a baking tray and bake for 20 minutes in the preheated oven until golden brown.

11. **Serve**: Let rest for 2 minutes (careful: hot!) and serve.
